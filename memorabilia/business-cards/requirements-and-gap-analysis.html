4. ROOT CAUSE OF FAILURE

I made ONE fundamental wrong assumption and never corrected it:

I assumed "dark cards look premium" and forced a #0a0a0a black background on EVERY design.

This is the opposite of what GlassBox stands for. GlassBox = glass = transparent = LIGHT. Every card should have been on a white/frosted/translucent background from the start.

Secondary failures:

  • Abbreviated contacts "wa" "li" "gh" — nobody knows what these mean. Should be full words.
  • Back sides too sparse — wasted space, should carry useful information.
  • No actual glass texture — the card background itself should feel like glass, not just the logo.
  • Didn't listen to "screaming transparency" — the entire card should communicate openness at first glance.
