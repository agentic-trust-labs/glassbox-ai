🔬 Research: Open-Source Business Card Best Practices

What open-source maintainers put on their cards

  • GitHub handle/repo URL is #1 - this is the open-source calling card. Most prominent element after name.
  • QR code linking to repo - many OSS devs use QR codes. Instant access, no typing.
  • "Contributor welcome" or "Open for collaboration" - explicit invitation to contribute.
  • Minimal personal info - name, one social link (usually GitHub), maybe Twitter/X.
  • Project tagline - short, memorable, tells people what the project does.
  • IIT/credential mention - adds trust especially in India. Subtle placement (back or small text) avoids looking boastful.

Design principles from research

  • Max 2-3 fonts - we use Inter (brand) + JetBrains Mono (code). Clean pairing.
  • Color psychology - Green = growth, nature, trust. White = cleanliness, transparency. Black = elegance.
  • Spot UV on logo - makes the butterfly "glow" physically on the card. Very premium at Rs 3-5/card extra.
  • 350 GSM + matte lamination - industry standard. Dark cards NEED matte to avoid fingerprints.
  • Don't overcrowd - business card is a conversation starter, not a resume. Max 7 items on front.
  • Font minimum 7pt - anything smaller won't print legibly. Our 0.32rem ~ 7.5pt at 300 DPI.
  • Back side = brand reinforcement - logo large, tagline, maybe repo URL. Not contact info.

The "GlassBox not BlackBox" signal

  • NASSCOM feedback: "GlassBox" as a word resonated. "Not BlackBox" got a reaction.
  • This is a contrast frame - people remember what something is NOT. Very effective in branding.
  • Best placement: front side as a secondary line, or back side as a hero element.
  • The crossed-out "BlackBox" creates visual tension that draws the eye.
